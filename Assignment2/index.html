Causes and Impact of Air Pollution

Air Pollution is one of the world's largest health and environmental problems that attributed to 11.65% of deaths globally. Almost all of the global population (99%) are exposed to air pollution levels that put them at risk for diseases, such as cancer, pneumonia and etc. There has been a record of 6.7 million deaths each year from exposure to ambient and household air pollution and a 2.4 billion people primarily rely on polluting fuels and technologies for cooking.

How is Air Pollution affected Globally?

The highest PM2.5 index recorded in 2017 is from Niger of 94.05 which is significantly higher than the rest of the world. Reported that the air pollution in Niger was caused by natural factors such as dust, sand, emmision of cars, industry waste, and more. Furthermore, countries such as India, Egypt and Saudi Arabia falls in to the few top countries with the highest PM2.5 index value.

Fortunately, country like india aims to reduce particulate matter emissions by much as 30% from 2017 levels by 2024.
